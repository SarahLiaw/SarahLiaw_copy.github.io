Quantification of Predictive Uncertainty with Scalable-Uncertainty Aware Models

In the context of exact GP inferences, DKL models usually suffer from poor scalability in the size N of the dataset due to the O(N3) computational complexity of GPs. Additionally, exact GP inferences are only possible for Gaussian likelihoods, making approximation necessary for classification tasks. To address both issues, Ober and Rasmussen introduced the use of stochastic variational inferences for GPs, leading to the development of stochastic variational deep kernel learning (SVDKL). As part of the CS159 (Uncertainty Quantification) project, we propose frameworks with SVDKL as an improvement to DKL to quantify uncertainty estimates in medical classification tasks and show the importance of lower dimensional representations in scalability for GPs.

Validation of M-SEPS Algorithm (v2) for CASTOR

M-SEPS or MultiSpacecraft Simultaneous Estimation of Pose and Shape algorithm is a reliable, novel approach to estimate the pose of an unknown or uncooperative space target using monocular vision, or monocular cameras. In M-SEPS, a set of chasers with monocular cameras exchange information over some network to estimate the relative orientation and degree of freedoms (rotational, translational, etc.) in order to approximate the relative kinematic status of the target.

A Comprehensive Examination of Games: Modelling with Cumulative Probability and (Absorbing) Markov Chains

Snakes and Ladders is a popular board game played on a 10x10 grid. Players roll a die to move and aim to reach the final square (square 100) first. Snakes and ladders introduce complexity by moving players further or closer to the final square respectively. The mathematical complexities arising from these elements are not considered within Markov theory. The question remains: Do ladders accelerate players' progress in the game? The objective of my investigation is to analyze the application of Markov Chains principles in evaluating the impact of ladders on the expected number of moves required for a player to win a game of Snakes and Ladders. Using a fair six-sided die, I will examine how the ratio of ladders to snakes affects the game dynamics.
