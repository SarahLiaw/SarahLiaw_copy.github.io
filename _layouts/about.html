Hi, I'm Sarah.

I'm currently a rising Junior at Caltech majoring in Computer Science (and Mathematics... previously an Applied and Computational Maths major), advised by Professor Steven Low. I like solving problems in mathematical settings and finding real-world applications for my work. I have had the opportunity to work with amazing mentors including Professor Pietro Perona, Professor Aadel Chaudhuri (WUSTL), Professor Soon-Jo Chung, and currently, I am working under Dr. Ricardo Baptista to further develop research in approximations of nonlinear manifolds, density estimation, probabilistic graphical models, and learning their structure with minimal assumptions on their distribution.

Outside of work, I have been actively involved in various committees at Caltech, including the Academic and Research Committee and the Board of Control Committee. I am also a member of the dance club, where I participated in the 2023 Dance Show! Additionally, I enjoy playing the piano and badminton and am also a member of the Caltech Data Science Club. Recently, I have also started to play chess, and am finding it to be a challenging and engaging experience.

In the past, I had the privilege of being a member of Malaysia's Asian-Pacific Informatics Olympiad team. More recently, I gained valuable experience working with Dr. Matt Todd at University College London (UCL) in the MycetOS initiative, focusing on the synthesis of novel molecules for neglected diseases like Mycetoma. This experience has significantly shaped my passion for drug discovery, particularly in the realm of computation and open-source research.

These diverse experiences have shaped both my perspectives and my goals. Currently, my interests lie in algorithms (specifically in queueing theory and game theory), machine learning, computational drug discovery, and statistical learning. In addition, I am also open to quantitative experiences in industry, particularly in the areas of computational work in finance or drug synthesis. I am fascinated by the application of computational methods and mathematical models in these fields, which can contribute to more efficient and effective decision-making processes.
